# marketing-plan.md

## Positioning
**Primary Positioning Statement:**  
"Code-Cleanser: Your AI-powered partner for simplifying complex codebases and boosting developer productivity."

**Alternative Positioning Statements:**  
1. "Transform tangled code into clean, maintainable structures with Code-Cleanser."
2. "Unlock developer efficiency by refactoring complex codebases effortlessly with Code-Cleanser."
3. "Code-Cleanser: Simplifying your code, amplifying your productivity."

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)
1. **Persona 1: Alex the Senior Developer**  
   - **Daily Job:** Works on maintaining and refactoring legacy systems in a mid-sized tech company.  
   - **Budget:** $1,500/month for productivity tools.  

2. **Persona 2: Jamie the Tech Lead**  
   - **Daily Job:** Oversees a team of developers, focusing on code quality and team efficiency.  
   - **Budget:** $2,000/month for team productivity solutions.  

3. **Persona 3: Morgan the Software Architect**  
   - **Daily Job:** Designs software architecture and ensures code maintainability across projects.  
   - **Budget:** $2,500/month for tools that enhance code quality and team collaboration.  

## Channels
1. **Developer Forums (e.g., Stack Overflow, Reddit)**  
   - **CAC Estimate:** $50 per acquired customer through targeted ads and community engagement.  

2. **Content Marketing (Blog Posts, Tutorials, Webinars)**  
   - **CAC Estimate:** $75 per acquired customer through SEO-optimized content and lead magnets.  

3. **Partnerships with Coding Bootcamps and Online Courses**  
   - **CAC Estimate:** $100 per acquired customer through affiliate programs and co-marketing efforts.  

## Content Cadence
- **Week 1:** Launch blog post introducing Code-Cleanser; share on social media.  
- **Week 2:** Publish a tutorial on using Code-Cleanser for legacy code refactoring.  
- **Week 3:** Host a webinar featuring a live demo of Code-Cleanser.  
- **Week 4:** Release a case study showcasing early user success stories.  
- **Week 5:** Share user testimonials and feedback on social media.  
- **Week 6:** Publish an article on the importance of code quality and maintainability.  
- **Week 7:** Create an infographic summarizing the benefits of using Code-Cleanser.  
- **Week 8:** Launch a Q&A session on developer forums to address questions about Code-Cleanser.  
- **Week 9:** Release a video tutorial on advanced features of Code-Cleanser.  
- **Week 10:** Publish a blog post on future updates and roadmap for Code-Cleanser.  
- **Week 11:** Share user-generated content and success stories.  
- **Week 12:** Conduct a survey to gather feedback and suggestions for improvements.  

## Launch Milestones
- **D-30:** Finalize product features and prepare marketing materials.  
- **D-0:** Official launch of Code-Cleanser; initiate marketing campaigns across selected channels.  
- **D+30:** Analyze initial user feedback; release a minor update based on feedback.  
- **D+90:** Review performance metrics; plan for next feature set based on user needs and market trends.  

## Success Metrics
- **Daily Active Users (DAU):** Target 500 DAUs by D+90.  
- **Monthly Recurring Revenue ($MRR):** Target $10,000 MRR by D+90.
